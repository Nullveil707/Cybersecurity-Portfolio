# Risk Register

This project was completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Certificate. The activity focused on evaluating different risks that could affect a commercial bank and assigning scores based on likelihood, severity, and priority.

For this risk register, I reviewed several risks related to the bank’s funds, including business email compromise, a compromised user database, financial records exposure, theft, and supply chain disruption. Each risk was scored using the formula:

`Likelihood × Severity = Priority`

This helped me practice how security teams compare risks and decide which issues should be addressed first. Risks with higher priority scores, such as business email compromise and financial records leaks, would require faster attention because they could cause serious financial, operational, and regulatory impacts.

This project helped me better understand how risk assessments support cybersecurity planning. By organizing risks in a register, security teams can make more informed decisions about where to focus resources and how to reduce potential harm to an organization.
